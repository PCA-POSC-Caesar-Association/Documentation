---
title: Content status definitions (aligned to the PCA review process)
---
This page explains what each **status** means, and where it fits in the **PCA Content Review Process** (Submission → Automatic QA → Community review → Publication). Complete documentation of the **PCA Content Review Process** is available on our [homepage](https://www.posccaesar.org/pages/documentation).

## Draft

**Meaning:** Work-in-progress content that is not yet submitted for PCA review.  
**In the process:** _Before_ Submission. (Internal preparation / collaboration, not yet facilitated in the platform.)

## Submitted

**Meaning:** Content has passed **Automatic QA**, been created with an IRI in the platform, and is now waiting for **Working Group** community review.  
**In the process:** After Automatic QA, _during_ Community review.

## Approved

**Meaning:** The Working Group has accepted the content as industry-ready (approval decision recorded with justification and dependency impact).  
**In the process:** Outcome of Community review, reflected during Publication.

## Rejected

**Meaning:** The Working Group has decided **not** to approve the content (decision recorded with justification and dependency impact).  
**In the process:** Outcome of Community review, reflected during Publication.

## Deprecated

**Meaning:** Content is still kept for traceability, but it has been **superseded by a newer version** (linked using **Replaces** / “is replaced by”).  
**In the process:** Happens _after_ publication when a new version is introduced, or _before_ Community Review if a new version is created before it was reviewed.

## Externally Managed

**Meaning:** PCA’s digital representation of content managed outside of PCA.  
**In the process:** Not part of PCA’s submission/review lifecycle itself; setup on a case basis.
